HaysMac: Turning one expert conversation into a series of LinkedIn videos

A professional services video project filmed in HaysMac's new office overlooking the Thames, then edited into a longer conversation piece and multiple short-form LinkedIn clips.

Discuss a video project
Client HaysMac
Sector Professional services / accountancy
Location London
Deliverables Long-form conversation video, short LinkedIn edits, hook-led social clips
Focus Expert-led content, LinkedIn engagement, campaign assets

The brief

HaysMac wanted to create a conversation-led video in their new office space, with the aim of producing a longer piece of content alongside shorter edits for LinkedIn.

The goal was to make the conversation feel professional and considered, while still keeping it natural and easy to watch. Each shorter clip needed to work as a standalone post, with a clear topic, a strong opening and a reason for viewers to keep watching.

Filming in a new office overlooking the Thames

The shoot took place in HaysMac's new office space overlooking the Thames, giving the video a strong sense of place and making the setting feel specific to them.

The main challenge was balancing the bright window light behind the speakers with the indoor lighting on their faces. The setup needed to keep the view visible without leaving the subjects underexposed.

I used a lean interview setup with additional lighting and clean audio capture, keeping the shoot professional while still allowing the conversation to feel natural.

The longer conversation

The full edit gave HaysMac a complete conversation piece that could support the wider campaign, while also providing the foundation for a series of shorter social edits.

Repurposing the conversation for LinkedIn

Rather than treating the shoot as one finished video, I worked through the conversation to identify the strongest lines, clearest themes and most useful standalone moments.

These were then shaped into shorter LinkedIn edits, each built around a specific hook or question. This gave HaysMac several pieces of content from one shoot, instead of relying on a single post.

Short-form LinkedIn edits

Question-led hook

A shorter LinkedIn edit using the opening question to create immediate context and pull viewers into the answer.

Key insight edit

A focused clip built around one of the strongest business points from the wider conversation.

Quickfire topic edit

A shorter standalone video using a punchier moment from the conversation to create a more social-first piece of content.

Campaign support clip

An additional edit designed to give the marketing team another useful asset for LinkedIn.

From one shoot to a content bank

A single filming session gave HaysMac a library of video content they could use across LinkedIn over weeks, rather than a single post that would disappear from feeds after a day.

This approach makes the most of the time and budget put into a shoot, turning one conversation into a structured set of assets with different uses and different audiences.

  • One shoot produced multiple finished videos
  • Each clip was built around a specific hook or question
  • Longer edit supported the wider campaign alongside shorter posts
  • Content was designed to work independently, not just as a series
  • Gave the marketing team a bank of ready-to-use LinkedIn assets

Why this works for professional services

For professional services firms, video content works best when it helps people hear directly from the experts inside the business.

A longer conversation gives space for proper insight, while shorter LinkedIn edits make the content easier to share, easier to watch and more useful for ongoing marketing.

It gives firms a way to create regular, people-led content without needing a completely new idea for every post.
